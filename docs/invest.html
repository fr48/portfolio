Lede: How Ransomware Impacts Regular People

Ransomware doesn't discriminate. Over the past year, it has hit individuals, businesses, and municipalities. As the targets biggers, so have the ransom demands.

Incidents against cities have skyrocketed...from just two confirmed reports against cities in 2013 to 54 in 2018. There have already been 77 confirmed reports against city and county governments in the United States in the first eight months of 2019. When municipal governments get infected with ransomware, the employees are the first ones impacted: the phone systems may go down, email is no longer available, and the website goes offline. Payroll departments can't access their applications, so city employees can't get paid.
